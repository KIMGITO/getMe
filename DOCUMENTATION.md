# 🚀 getME — Master Product Documentation

## Project Lead: Dennis Kimanthi  
## Version: 1.0  
## Stack: Laravel, React, Flutter  

---

getME is a modern hyper-local logistics and shopping assistance ecosystem designed to solve everyday urban delivery and errand challenges within Kenya’s rapidly growing cities and towns. The system is developed by Dennis Kimanthi as a full-stack digital infrastructure that integrates real-time logistics, secure financial systems, identity verification, and mobile-first applications into one unified platform. The core purpose of getME is to bridge the gap between customers who need quick access to goods and services and motorcycle riders who form the backbone of last-mile delivery across urban and semi-urban regions in Kenya.

The idea behind getME originates from the informal nature of errands in many Kenyan cities. In places such as Nairobi, Mombasa, Nakuru, Kisumu, and Eldoret, people frequently rely on boda-boda riders for daily tasks such as grocery shopping, parcel delivery, medicine collection, and general errands. However, these interactions are often conducted through phone calls, WhatsApp messages, or verbal agreements, which lack structure, accountability, and security. There is no standardized system for tracking deliveries, verifying purchases, ensuring fair payments, or protecting both parties in case of disputes. This creates a trust gap that has existed for years within the informal logistics sector.

getME is designed to eliminate this trust gap by introducing a fully digital and structured ecosystem that formalizes these informal errands into secure transactions. The platform is not built like traditional delivery applications that rely on fixed inventories, structured supermarkets, or restaurant menus. Instead, it is specifically designed for Kenya’s open-market economy where prices fluctuate dynamically, goods are sourced from informal vendors, and shopping requests are flexible and often customized by the customer. This makes getME fundamentally different because it reflects how real-life shopping actually happens in most parts of the country.

The system operates through three main categories of users: Buyers, Riders, and Administrators. Buyers represent customers who create shopping or delivery requests through the platform. They are able to specify what they need, set estimated budgets, choose preferred markets or locations, and monitor the entire delivery process in real time. Riders are the delivery agents responsible for physically executing these errands. They receive job requests, travel to markets or shops, purchase requested items, and deliver them to customers. Administrators act as the control layer of the system, managing users, verifying identities, monitoring transactions, resolving disputes, and ensuring that the entire ecosystem operates securely and efficiently.

getME is built using a modern and scalable technology stack consisting of Laravel for backend services, React for the web-based administrative dashboard, and Flutter for cross-platform mobile applications. Laravel serves as the core engine that manages business logic, authentication, payment processing, and real-time communication. React is used to build a fast and responsive web interface for administrators, while Flutter powers mobile applications used by both Buyers and Riders. This combination ensures that the platform remains scalable, maintainable, and capable of supporting high volumes of real-time transactions.

One of the most important innovations in getME is the integration of escrow-based payments. Instead of sending money directly to riders before a task begins, buyers deposit funds into the platform’s secure wallet system using M-Pesa STK Push integration. These funds are held safely until the delivery is completed and verified. This eliminates common issues such as riders disappearing with money or disputes over incomplete deliveries. The escrow system ensures that financial trust is enforced at a system level rather than relying on verbal agreements between users.

In addition to escrow, getME introduces the Handshake Security Protocol, which is a digital verification system designed to confirm successful delivery. When a rider arrives at the customer’s location, the system generates a unique four-digit PIN that the buyer must provide. The rider enters this PIN into the application to confirm completion of the order. Only after this verification does the system release payment to the rider. This ensures that physical delivery has actually taken place and prevents fraudulent claims of completion.

The security architecture of getME extends beyond payments. The system includes identity verification for riders using National ID, driving licenses, KRA PINs, and Sacco membership validation. This ensures that only verified individuals can operate on the platform. GPS tracking is continuously used during active deliveries, allowing buyers and administrators to monitor rider movement in real time. Geofencing alerts are also implemented to detect unusual deviations from expected delivery routes, which helps prevent theft or unauthorized behavior.

Another major component of getME is its real-time infrastructure. The system uses WebSocket technology, specifically Laravel Reverb, to enable live updates for GPS tracking, order status changes, and instant notifications. Firebase Cloud Messaging is used to push alerts to mobile devices, ensuring that users receive updates instantly without needing to refresh the application. This real-time architecture is essential for maintaining transparency and smooth communication between all parties involved in a delivery.

The platform also introduces a Safety and Insurance layer that enhances trust and reduces risk. getME includes an internal micro-insurance system funded by a portion of the platform service fees. This creates a Loss Reserve Fund that can be used to refund buyers in cases of verified disputes, lost goods, or delivery failures. This internal insurance model removes dependency on external insurance providers and allows for faster resolution of claims.

To strengthen accountability further, riders are required to capture receipts and photos of purchased goods before leaving the market. This creates a digital proof-of-purchase system that documents the condition and existence of goods at the point of purchase. In case of disputes, administrators can compare purchase records, GPS logs, and delivery evidence to determine what actually happened during the transaction.

getME also includes an emergency reporting system for both buyers and riders. Buyers can trigger a safety alert if a rider behaves suspiciously or deviates from the expected route. This alert sends rider identity information, GPS location, and order details to administrators and security teams. Riders also have their own emergency feature that allows them to report unsafe delivery environments, triggering alerts to nearby riders and administrators. This creates a shared safety network within the ecosystem.

From a financial perspective, getME operates on multiple revenue streams. The platform takes a commission from each delivery, charges a service fee to cover operational costs, and introduces premium delivery options for urgent requests. Merchant partnerships also generate additional revenue, while the escrow system allows efficient float management that supports liquidity within the platform. A portion of service fees is allocated to the insurance fund, ensuring continuous protection for users.

The operational workflow of getME is structured to ensure simplicity and transparency. A buyer creates a request, deposits funds into escrow, and nearby riders are notified. A rider accepts the job, purchases the required items, uploads receipts, and proceeds to delivery. The buyer tracks the rider in real time and confirms delivery using the Handshake PIN. Once confirmed, the system releases payment to the rider and closes the transaction. Every step in this process is digitally recorded to ensure accountability.

getME is designed with scalability in mind. The architecture supports future expansion into additional services such as food delivery, pharmacy logistics, corporate deliveries, AI-powered rider allocation, micro-loans for riders, and insurance integrations. The system is built to scale across Kenya and eventually expand into the broader East African market.

In conclusion, getME represents a new generation of logistics infrastructure tailored specifically for the realities of Kenya’s urban economy. It is not just a delivery application but a trust-driven ecosystem that integrates technology, finance, and logistics into a unified platform. By combining escrow payments, real-time tracking, identity verification, and insurance-backed safety mechanisms, getME transforms informal errands into structured, secure, and reliable digital transactions. Built by Dennis Kimanthi using Laravel, React, and Flutter, the platform aims to redefine how hyper-local logistics operate across East Africa by introducing transparency, accountability, and trust at every level of interaction.
